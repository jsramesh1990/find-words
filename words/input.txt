"Puzzle words" can refer to terms used to describe puzzle mechanics, common words found within crossword answers, or high-frequency sight words used in educational games.
 
Common Puzzle Mechanics & Terminology
These terms are frequently used in the context of playing or designing puzzle and escape room games. 

    Anagram: A word or phrase formed by rearranging the letters of another (e.g., "cinema" from "iceman").
    Bingo: In Spelling Bee, using all seven available letters to start at least one word.
    Cipher: A method of encoding a message, such as the Caesar Cipher (shifting letters) or Substitution Cipher.
    Conundrum: A confusing or difficult problem or question.
    Pangram: A word that uses every letter from a given set at least once.
    Rebus: A puzzle in which words are represented by combinations of pictures and individual letters.
    Stumper: A particularly challenging puzzle or question. 

High-Frequency "Crosswordese"
Certain short words appear frequently in crossword puzzles due to their vowel-heavy compositions. 

    ALEE: Toward the side of a vessel sheltered from the wind.
    ASEA: At sea; out into the open water.
    EMU: A large, flightless Australian bird.
    ERATO: The Greek Muse of lyric poetry.
    SMEE: Captain Hook's bumbling sidekick in Peter Pan. 

Educational "Puzzle Words" (Sight Words)
In Montessori education, "puzzle words" are high-frequency sight words that cannot be easily sounded out phonetically. 

    Level 1-2: I, a, the, he, she, me, we, be.
    Level 3-4: is, as, his, has, from, mother, brother, son, of.
    Level 5-6: some, come, done, none, one, give, live, have, love.
    Level 7-8: to, do, move, prove, could, should, would.
